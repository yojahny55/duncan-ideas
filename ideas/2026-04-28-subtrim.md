## Problem Statement

The average knowledge worker now pays for **12-20 digital subscriptions** — SaaS tools, AI services, streaming, cloud storage, premium apps. A 2026 TechRadar survey found nearly half of Gen Z want to live in the past due to "too many subscriptions." Businesses are bleeding too: Uber reportedly burned through its entire 2026 AI budget in 4 months.

People **don't know what they're paying for**. Subscriptions auto-renew silently. Duplicate tools pile up. Free trials convert without notice. AI agent costs are spiraling. There's no single dashboard that shows the bleeding.

## Solution

**SubTrim** connects to your bank/credit card via Plaid, uses AI to categorize every recurring charge, and surfaces:

1. **Waste detection** — duplicate tools (3 AI writing assistants?), unused subscriptions (last charge but no login in 90 days)
2. **Cost forecasting** — "You're on track to spend $4,200 on subscriptions this year, up 38% from last year"
3. **Smarter alternatives** — "You're paying $20/mo for Tool A and $15/mo for Tool B. Tool C does both for $12/mo"
4. **AI spend guardrails** — specifically tracks AI/API usage costs with alerts before budget blowouts
5. **One-click cancel** — generates cancelation emails/flows for each detected subscription

### Key Differentiator
Unlike existing subscription trackers (Rocket Money, Truebill/Subtake), SubTrim focuses heavily on **AI tool and API spending** — the fastest-growing and least-understood cost category in 2026. It also detects token waste patterns across AI services.

## Target Users

- **Freelancers & solopreneurs** (5-15 subscriptions, $200-800/mo)
- **Small business owners** (managing team tool sprawl)
- **Developers & AI builders** (tracking API costs across OpenAI, Anthropic, Google, etc.)
- **Startups** (burn rate visibility for investor reporting)
