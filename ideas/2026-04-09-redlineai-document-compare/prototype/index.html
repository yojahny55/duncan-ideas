AI Analysis

⚠️ Proceed with Caution

This "minor update" contains 3 significant changes that will cost you money and restrict your flexibility:

  1. New pet fee: $200/month per pet. This wasn't in the original. If you have pets, this is a major new expense — $2,400-4,800/year.
  2. Notice period doubled: 30 → 60 days. You need to decide about moving out 2 months in advance instead of 1. This makes it harder to be flexible.
  3. Deposit return window doubled: 14 → 30 days. In many states (CA: 21 days, MA: 30 days, FL: 15 days), there are legal limits on how long a landlord can hold your deposit. Check your state law.

Recommendation: Do NOT sign as-is. The pet fee and notice period changes are substantial. Consider negotiating: ask to keep the 30-day notice, reduce the pet fee to $100/month, or cap the deposit return at your state's legal limit.
