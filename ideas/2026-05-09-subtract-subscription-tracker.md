# Subtract — The Anti-Subscription Subscription Tracker

**Date:** 2026-05-09
**Status:** Idea
**Inspiration:** Reddit threads on subscription fatigue — users drowning in recurring charges, forgetting what they pay for, and resenting every app demanding $9.99/mo.

---

## Problem Statement

People are hemorrhaging money on subscriptions they don't use, forgot about, or signed up for a "free trial" and never canceled. The average person has **12+ active subscriptions** totaling $200-400/month. Most can't list them all from memory. Banks show transactions but don't connect the dots. Existing trackers require manual entry — which defeats the point, because the people who need this most are too disorganized to maintain it.

**The core pain:** "I know I'm wasting money on subscriptions but I don't know which ones or how to stop."

## Solution

**Subtract** — a zero-entry subscription tracker that auto-discovers your recurring charges from bank/credit card data, categorizes them, shows you exactly what you're paying, and gives you one-tap cancellation paths (or generates cancelation emails/letters for you).

### Key Features
1. **Auto-discovery** — Plaid/bank sync detects recurring charges by pattern (same amount, same merchant, recurring interval). No manual entry.
2. **Subscription health score** — Grades your spend: green (worth it), yellow (underutilized), red (unused 30+ days). Integrates with usage data where possible.
3. **One-tap cancel assist** — Pre-fills cancellation emails, links directly to cancel pages, or provides step-by-step cancel instructions per service.
4. **Savings challenges** — "Cancel 3 subscriptions this month, redirect $47 to savings." Gamified reduction.
5. **Share cost** — Detects subscriptions that support family sharing and suggests splitting with household members.
6. **Price change alerts** — Notifies when a subscription price increases.

## Target Users

- **Primary:** 25-45 year olds with 5+ subscriptions, moderate financial anxiety
- **Secondary:** Freelancers/small business owners tracking SaaS sprawl
- **Tertiary:** Parents managing family plans (Netflix, Spotify, iCloud, etc.)

## Tech Stack

| Layer | Choice | Why |
|-------|--------|-----|
| Frontend | React Native + Expo | Cross-platform, one codebase |
| Backend | Node.js + Fastify | Lightweight, fast |
| Database | PostgreSQL + Redis | Relational for transactions, cache for sessions |
| Bank Data | Plaid API | Industry standard, 12k+ institutions |
| Auth | Clerk or Firebase Auth | Quick setup, social login |
| Email Generation | OpenAI API (structured output) | Cancel letter/email drafting |
| Hosting | Vercel (frontend) + Railway (API) | Simple deploy, scales affordably |
| Notifications | OneSignal | Push notifications for price alerts |

## Monetization

1. **Freemium** — Free tier tracks up to 10 subscriptions. Premium ($2.99/mo) for unlimited + cancel assist + price alerts.
2. **Referral bounties** — Affiliate commissions when users switch to recommended alternatives (e.g., "cancel Netflix, try this free alternative"). Ethical: always disclose.
3. **Savings split** — Optional: redirect "saved" money to a high-yield savings account, take a small interchange fee. (Long-term play.)
4. **B2B API** — Sell anonymized subscription trend data to fintech companies. (Opt-in only.)

**Key pricing principle:** Subtract itself must NOT be a predatory subscription. $2.99/mo max. Annual discount. Free tier genuinely useful.

## Differentiation

- **Vs. Rocket Money / Truebill:** Subtract is privacy-first (no selling data), cheaper, open about monetization, and actually helps you cancel instead of just listing.
- **Vs. manual spreadsheets:** Zero entry. Auto-discovery is the killer feature.
- **Vs. bank "subscription features":** Banks show transactions. Subtract shows *intent* — are you using what you pay for?

## MVP Scope (v0.1)

1. Plaid integration → auto-detect recurring charges
2. Dashboard with subscription list, monthly total, health score
3. One-tap "help me cancel" with pre-filled email template
4. Push notification for price changes
