# SubAudit — Subscription Fatigue Killer

**Date:** 2026-05-16
**Source Inspiration:** Reddit r/CustomerSuccess, NYT "Subscription Fatigue Is Setting In" (May 12, 2026), Today Show segment (May 15) — 52% of US consumers report subscription fatigue. Subscription creep is a universal pain point.

---

## Problem Statement

People are bleeding money on forgotten subscriptions. The average American has **12+ active subscriptions** totaling $200-400/month, and routinely forgets about 2-3 of them. There's no single place to see what you're paying for, when it renews, whether you actually use it, or if there's a cheaper alternative. Bank statements are opaque — charges show up as "STRM*SERV800-555" nonsense. People feel powerless and ripped off.

**Key pain points:**
- "I found a meditation app I forgot I had for 8 months" — real quote from Medium article
- Streaming services keep raising prices silently
- No easy way to cancel (dark patterns everywhere)
- No awareness of duplicate services (3 cloud storage subscriptions?)
- Car features now being subscription-gated (BMW heated seats, etc.)

## Solution

**SubAudit** — a subscription intelligence dashboard that scans your bank/credit card transactions, identifies all recurring charges, and gives you an honest audit of your subscription life.

### Core Features
1. **Auto-Discovery** — Connect bank accounts via Plaid, automatically detect recurring charges (AI-powered merchant name resolution)
2. **Subscription Health Score** — Per-subscription usage estimate vs. cost, color-coded red/yellow/green
3. **Kill Switch** — One-tap cancellation letters/emails with pre-filled templates (cancel-for-me as a service)
4. **Price Watch** — Alerts when a subscription raises its price, tracks price history
5. **Duplication Detector** — "You're paying for Spotify AND Apple Music AND YouTube Premium — pick one"
6. **Free Alternative Finder** — Suggests free/cheaper alternatives for each subscription
7. **Annual Audit Report** — Beautiful shareable report: "I saved $847 this year by cutting subscriptions"

### Magic Moment
Onboarding: "You have $347/month in subscriptions. We found $89 in duplicates and forgotten services. Tap to save."

## Tech Stack

| Layer | Choice | Why |
|-------|--------|-----|
| Frontend | **React Native + Next.js** | Cross-platform mobile + web from one codebase (Expo) |
| Backend | **Node.js + Fastify** | Fast, Yojahny's wheelhouse |
| Bank Integration | **Plaid API** | Industry standard, reliable transaction data |
| AI/NLP | **OpenAI GPT-4o-mini** | Merchant name resolution, subscription classification |
| Database | **PostgreSQL + Redis** | Transactional data + caching |
| Auth | **Clerk** | Quick auth, supports social login |
| Hosting | **Vercel (frontend) + Railway (API)** | Fast deploy, scales well |
| Payments | **Stripe** | Subscriptions for our own subscription (ironic but necessary) |

## Monetization

1. **Freemium** — Free for up to 5 subscriptions. $4.99/month or $39.99/year for unlimited.
2. **Audit Report** — One-time $2.99 annual report for free users (gateway to subscription).
3. **Affiliate** — Referral fees when users switch to recommended alternatives.
4. **Enterprise** — Teams version for small businesses tracking SaaS sprawl ($12/seat/month).

**Unit economics:** Plaid costs ~$0.50/user for connection. If average user saves $50/month, $4.99 is a no-brainer. Payback in first month.
