Line-by-Line Feedback

⚠️

"Hi Sarah,"

Too casual for a work email. Use "Dear Sarah," or just start with your message.

⚠️

"I was wondering if you might have a moment to maybe look at my project?"

"Wondering if you might" + "maybe" = double qualifier. You sound unsure. Try: "Could you review my project?"

🚩

"I know you're super busy so no worries if not."

You're giving Sarah an out before she even responds. Don't apologize for asking.

💡

"It would be really great if you could."

What's in it for Sarah? How will this help her? Add context.

Better Versions

Confident

Hi Sarah, Could you spare 15 minutes to review my project? I'd value your feedback on the technical approach. Thanks, John

Persuasive

Hi Sarah, I've been working on a project that ties into your team's recent work on X. Would you have time to review it? Your input would be incredibly valuable for the next phase. Best, John

Direct

Hi Sarah, I'd like your feedback on my project. When are you available this week for a 15-minute review? Thanks, John
